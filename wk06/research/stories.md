## Story S1: Notification system
**Situation**: When I'm busy and not on the app  but still need to be aware of what tasks I need to complete.
**Motivation**: I want the app to send me a notification about important tasks I need to complete soon.
**Outcome**: So I can stay on top of my tasks without needing to always check the app and be reminded of what I need to do.
**Underlying need**: Because helps people remeber what tasks they need to do, especially people with memory issues. Also helps people
with motor issues not need to constantly access app if difficult for them.

**Evidence**: Participant A Q6
**Inclusion risk**: Cognitive, motor
**Type**: Job story
**WCAG**: 4.1.3 Status Messages (AA)

---

## Story S2: Priority
**Situation**: When I have a lot of tasks I want to give them a priority rank that I can sort them by.
**Motivation**: I want to know what tasks I need to do most urgently, even when I have a lot of tasks.
**Outcome**: So I can keep track of my most important work I should be completing.
**Underlying need**: Because large lists can be difficult to keep track of. This could be especially challenging to user's with a screen reader who need to listen to each task and take time to listen for if its a high priority task or not.

**Evidence**: Participant B Q3
**Inclusion risk**: Cognitive, Screen reader
**Type**: Job story
**WCAG**: N/A

## Story S3: Complexity rankings
**Situation**: When I am tired I want to know what tasks I can complete easily based on their complexity ranking.
**Motivation**: I want to know what tasks I need to do that do not take too much energy.
**Outcome**: So I can keep still be productive when I feel fatigued.
**Underlying need**: Because even when I am tired I want to be productive without expensing too much energy.

**Evidence**: Participant A Q3, Participant C Q1
**Inclusion risk**: Cognitive, motor
**Type**: Job story
**WCAG**: N/A

---

## Story S4: Keyboard navigation
**Situation**: When my mouse or trackpad or unavailable.
**Motivation**: I want to be able to use the app with only a keyboard.
**Outcome**: So I can still be productive in situations where I only have access to a keyboard or prefer to only use a keyboard.
**Underlying need**: Because users with motor issues that struggle with a mouse or individuals who prefer to only use a keyboard should be accounted for.

**Evidence**: Participant B Q4, Participant C Q4
**Inclusion risk**: Motor impairment, keyboard preference
**Type**: Job story
**WCAG**: 2.1.1 Keyboard (A)

---

## Story S5: Dark mode
**Situation**: In bright situations or low light situations when dark mode is easier to look at.
**Motivation**: I want to be able to use the app in dark mode so it is comfortable to use in all enviroments.
**Outcome**: In bright sunlight app can be hard to see, dark mode could provide more contrast and make this better.
**Underlying need**: Low contrast makes app hard to use in direct sunlight and individuals with low vision will struggle with less contrast. As well as this plenty of people prefer using dark mode as it strains their eyes less in dark enviroments.

**Evidence**: Participant A Q5, Participant B Q5, Participant C Q5,
**Inclusion risk**: Low-vision, situational (bright light), prefers dark mode
**Type**: Job story
**WCAG**: 1.4.3 Contrast
---
## Story S6: Edit tasks
**Situation**: When I make a mistake typing a task or a task changes I want to be able to edit the task instead of recreating it myself.
**Motivation**: I want to be able to change details about a task faster than remaking it.
**Outcome**: Users would have an easy to alter details without deleting and making tasks.
**Underlying need**: Would allow users to fix small spelling mistakes, especially useful for dyslexic users or users with motor issues who struggle to type. 

**Evidence**: Participant A Q1, Participant B Q1 Q6
**Inclusion risk**: Users who struggle with reading and writing, users with motor issues that affect typing.
**Type**: Job story
**WCAG**: N/A
